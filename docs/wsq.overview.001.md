# Alpha, Beta, and the Problem of Skill

## 1. The Core Question

The fundamental problem is **not** “did we make money?”

The real question is:

> Are our returns due to  
> (1) the environment we operated in,  
> (2) genuine skill in our decision process, or  
> (3) luck?

Everything that follows exists to separate these three.

---

## 2. Returns Are Observations, Not Truth

We observe a time series of realized returns:

r_t

Each realized return is a **single draw** from a complex system that mixes:
- environment (market, regime, factors)
- strategy decisions
- randomness

A single return — or even a short sequence — is information-poor.

---

## 3. Beta = Environment (Context)

Beta represents **exposure to the environment**.

Key properties:
- It exists whether or not we want it.
- It can be intentional or accidental.
- It explains *where we were standing* when outcomes occurred.

“Rising tide raises all ships” is beta.

Beta is **not skill**.
Beta is **context**.

---

## 4. Alpha = What Remains After Conditioning on Environment

Alpha is not “extra return.”

Alpha is defined as:

> The expected return of the strategy **after the environment is neutralized**.

Formally:
- Alpha lives in expectation, not in samples.
- Alpha is conditional, not absolute.

If something behaves like beta, it is not alpha — it is dressed-up beta.

---

## 5. Expectation (𝔼) Is Where Skill Lives

The expectation operator (𝔼[·]) means:

> The long-run average outcome over repeated realizations of the same process.

Key distinction:

| Quantity | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 𝔼[·] | Property of the strategy / world |
| Sample mean | Property of observed history |

Skill must live in expectation.
One-off success is irrelevant.

---

## 6. Noise Is Unavoidable

After removing environment, what remains is:

- alpha (expected value)
- noise (random deviation)

Noise includes:
- timing randomness
- execution effects
- market microstructure
- regime transitions

Noise cannot be removed — only averaged down.

---

## 7. Signal-to-Noise as a Quality Measure

Alpha alone is meaningless without scale.

We measure **signal relative to noise**.

- Information Ratio (IR): quality of the signal
- Alpha t-stat: evidence that the signal is real

These are two dialects of the same idea:
> How strong is the signal compared to randomness?

---

## 8. Sample Size and Calibration

Finite samples distort perception.

Small samples:
- exaggerate luck
- inflate confidence
- mislead intuition

Reliable inference requires:
- sufficient data
- stability checks
- humility about uncertainty

We must calibrate expectations against sample size to avoid bias.

---

## 9. Latent Beta: The Alpha Impostor

Latent beta is exposure to **unknown or unmodeled factors**.

Symptoms:
- “Alpha” appears only in favorable regimes
- Drawdowns align with liquidity or volatility shocks
- Alpha collapses when a new factor is added

If a factor is real but not modeled, it leaks into alpha.

---

## 10. Two Ways to Detect Latent Beta

### A. Model-Based (Factor Expansion)
- Add candidate factors iteratively
- Observe whether alpha survives

### B. Behavior-Based (Regime Slicing)
- Examine performance across regimes
- Look for invariance rather than magnitude
- Test whether behavior differs from environment

The second approach emphasizes robustness over elegance.

---

## 11. Pearson Correlation as the Seed Idea

Pearson correlation answers:
> Do two things move together?

Regression and factor models are:
- directional
- scaled
- conditional extensions of Pearson

Alpha detection begins as “monster Pearson”  
and ends as **residual behavior under stress**.

---

## 12. Behavior Over Coefficients

True alpha should:
- degrade gracefully across regimes
- not flip sign erratically
- not disappear under modest perturbation

Behavioral similarity to the environment is evidence **against** alpha.

---

## 13. Non-Stationarity Changes the Meaning of Alpha

The world is not stationary.

Expectation becomes:
- local in time
- conditional on regime
- provisional rather than eternal

There is no “forever alpha” — only alpha that survives change longer than chance allows.

---

## 14. Structural vs Statistical Alpha

Two broad classes of edge:

### Statistical Alpha
- Pattern exploitation
- Correlation mining
- Often fragile and crowdable

### Structural Alpha
- Comes from constraints, frictions, forced flows
- More persistent, lower Sharpe, capacity-limited

Alpha is manufactured by positioning where others:
- cannot operate
- will not operate
- are forced to act

---

## 15. The Diagnostic Frame

Returns can be decomposed into:

- Good neighborhood (environment)
- Clever behavior (skill)
- Luck (noise)

Only one of these persists.

The purpose of alpha analysis is **triage**, not celebration.

---

## 16. Final Principle

Alpha is not a number.
Alpha is a property of a process.

If the world were flat, boring, and neutral:
> would this strategy still produce positive expectation?

That is the only question that matters.
